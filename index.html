We want to show that \begin{equation} \label{eq1} e^{\pi^{e^{\pi}}} > \pi^{e^{\pi^{e}}} (> 0) \end{equation} which is true if and only if \begin{equation} \pi^{e^{\pi}} > e^{\pi^{e}}ln(\pi) \end{equation} or equivalently \begin{equation} \frac{\pi^{e^{\pi}}}{e^{\pi^{e}}} > ln(\pi) \end{equation} is true. Now observe that \begin{equation} e^e > \pi \iff e > ln(\pi) \end{equation} If the first inequality is true in \begin{equation} \label{eq2} \frac{\pi^{e^{\pi}}}{e^{\pi^{e}}} > e > ln(\pi) \end{equation} then \eqref{eq1} is also true. \eqref{eq2} is equivalent to \begin{align} \pi^{e^{\pi}} > e \cdot e^{\pi^{e}} (>0)\\ e^{\pi} ln(\pi) > \pi^{e} + 1\\ \frac{e^{\pi}}{\pi^{e}} > \frac{\pi^{e} + 1}{\pi^{e} ln(\pi)} \label{qe4} \end{align} We know \( \frac{e^{\pi}}{\pi^{e}} > 1 \) and it seems very likely that \begin{equation} \label{eq3} \frac{e^{\pi}}{\pi^{e}} > 1 > \frac{\pi^{e} + 1}{\pi^{e} ln(\pi)} \end{equation} We now show that \eqref{qe4} holds by showing that \eqref{eq3} is true. Note that \begin{equation} \frac{\pi^{e} + 1}{\pi^{e} ln(\pi)} = \frac{1+\frac{1}{\pi^e}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{1+\frac{1}{3^e}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{1 + \frac{1}{3^{2.5}}}{ln(\pi)} \end{equation} \begin{equation} 3^{2.5} = x \iff x^2 = 3^5 = 243 \iff 15 < x < 16 \iff \frac{1}{3^{2.5}}< \frac{1}{15} \end{equation} \begin{equation} \frac{1 + \frac{1}{3^{2.5}}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{\frac{16}{15}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{\frac{16}{15}}{ln(3)} \end{equation} the problem now reduces to showing \( ln(3) > \frac{16}{15} \iff 3^{15} > e^{16} \)
\begin{align} 3^{15} = 243^3 = 59049 \cdot 243 > 59000 \cdot 243 = 14337700 \\ e^{16} < 2.8^{16} = 7.84^{8} = 61.4656^{4} < 61.5^{4} = 3782.25^{2} < 3783^{2} = 14311089 \end{align} so \( 3^{15} > e^{16} \) and thus \eqref{eq1} is indeed true. QED
