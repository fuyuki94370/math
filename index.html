We want to show that \begin{equation} \label{eq1} e^{\pi^{e^{\pi}}} > \pi^{e^{\pi^{e}}} (> 0) \end{equation} which is true if and only if \begin{equation} \pi^{e^{\pi}} > e^{\pi^{e}}ln(\pi) \end{equation} or equivalently \begin{equation} \frac{\pi^{e^{\pi}}}{e^{\pi^{e}}} > ln(\pi) \end{equation} is true. Now observe that \begin{equation} e^e > \pi \iff e > ln(\pi) \end{equation} If the first inequality is true in \begin{equation} \label{eq2} \frac{\pi^{e^{\pi}}}{e^{\pi^{e}}} > e > ln(\pi) \end{equation} then \eqref{eq1} is also true. \eqref{eq2} is equivalent to \begin{align} \pi^{e^{\pi}} > e \cdot e^{\pi^{e}} (>0)\\ e^{\pi} ln(\pi) > \pi^{e} + 1\\ \frac{e^{\pi}}{\pi^{e}} > \frac{\pi^{e} + 1}{\pi^{e} ln(\pi)} \label{qe4} \end{align} We know \( \frac{e^{\pi}}{\pi^{e}} > 1 \) and it seems very likely that \begin{equation} \label{eq3} \frac{e^{\pi}}{\pi^{e}} > 1 > \frac{\pi^{e} + 1}{\pi^{e} ln(\pi)} \end{equation} We now show that \eqref{qe4} holds by showing that \eqref{eq3} is true. Note that \begin{equation} \frac{\pi^{e} + 1}{\pi^{e} ln(\pi)} = \frac{1+\frac{1}{\pi^e}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{1+\frac{1}{3^e}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{1 + \frac{1}{3^{2.5}}}{ln(\pi)} \end{equation} \begin{equation} 3^{2.5} = x \iff x^2 = 3^5 = 243 \iff 15 < x < 16 \iff \frac{1}{3^{2.5}}< \frac{1}{15} \end{equation} \begin{equation} \frac{1 + \frac{1}{3^{2.5}}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{\frac{16}{15}}{ln(\pi)} < \frac{\frac{16}{15}}{ln(3)} \end{equation} the problem now reduces to showing \( ln(3) > \frac{16}{15} \iff 3^{15} > e^{16} \iff 15log(3) > 16log(e) \)
Now let \( x = log(3), 10^{x} = 3\) \begin{align} 10^{7x} = 3^7 = 2187 > 1000 = 10^3 \\ x = log(3) > \frac{3}{7} \\ 15log(3) > \frac{45}{7} > 6 \end{align} \begin{align} 16log(e) < 16log(2.8) = 16(2log(2) + log(7) - 1) \\ 10^{10log(2)} = 2^{10} = 1024 > 10^3 \\ log(2) > \frac{3}{10} \\ 10^{4log(7)} = 7^4 = 2401 > 10^3 \\ log(7) > \frac{3}{4} \\ 16log(e) < 16(2log(2) + log(7) - 1) = \frac{28}{5} < 6 \end{align} so \( 15log(3) > 6 > 16log(e) \) and thus \eqref{eq1} is indeed true.
